Our AI timeline.

  • Week One

    At the start of the camp, we initially wanted to create an AI that would generate job descriptions. We all liked this idea, but the data was not good enough because the data that we had had too many flaws and issues!

  • Beginning of Week Two

    Around the middle of the second week, we decided to set our sights on a joke generator instead. Unfortunately, once again, we had trouble with our data sets, this time because of the nonsensical and profane jokes that the AI would end up developing.

  • End of Week Two

    Finally, near the end of the second week, we changed our AI to become a TV script generator. Because of our experience from the previous two models, it took very little time to create this one. We decided that this model would be best, since there weren't any outstanding errors in our data, and there was a plethora of unique and useful data on the internet.

  • Week Three

    In the third week, we started making our website, which includes learning about and using HTML, CSS, and Flask. We decided to make a HTML page for each TV show, which believe makes the user experience better. There are many aspects to our website that we believe will help the user experience, which includes the drop down menus for the main characters. This will help prevent typos and ensure that the AI can generate its best code.
